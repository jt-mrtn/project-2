2021.002: DRACAENA

Similar to slip-scan photography, JavaScript can transform images by taking a column of pixels and reproduce them elsewhere. The effect is a smear of whatever color(s) happen to be hanging out in that column.

I've used this technique to form a moving background to a collage with a dragon-like font, which I think is appropriate to accompany images of draceana, aka the dragon tree.

Click any mouse button to see things a different way. There's a line to assist with your slip-scanning. The actual image being scanned is partially hidden but it is the repeated image you see I kid you not.

2021.003: SOME OF OZ

This project is an exploration of nonlinear narrative programming logic. It's like a game, sort of. You can type words or small phrases depending on where you'd like to go.

Fans of The Wizard of Oz will enjoy this little trip to the merry old land of Oz. As the title suggests, this is only some of the amazing fable that captured my imagination as a kid. You won't see any munchkins or Auntie Em, for example.

I spent a little extra time exploring different treatments of typography, going as far as using rotation in a for loop to provide a descending arc of text. I hope you enjoy it!
